# Southbank Towers

> A building is its people.

Southbank Towers is a resident portal for apartment buildings — a dignified, transparent place where residents can raise concerns, stay informed, and build the kind of neighbourliness that doesn't happen by accident.

The problem it solves is deceptively simple: right now, a resident with a noise complaint doesn't know whether to text the building manager, post in the WhatsApp group, or give up. The OC doesn't know what the building actually cares about. Information lives in inboxes. Accountability lives nowhere.

This app changes that.

---

## Who it serves

**Residents** are the majority user. They want three things: to be heard when something is wrong, to know what's happening in the building, and to feel like they belong to a community rather than just paying rent into a void. They are not technical. They are busy. If the app takes more than 30 seconds to do the obvious thing, they will go back to the WhatsApp group.

**The Owners Corporation (OC)** is a small group of elected volunteers who manage the building on everyone's behalf. They are drowning in emails, unsure what residents actually care about, and spend AGM prep weekends manually collating complaints from three different channels. They need visibility, accountability, and tools that make good governance feel less like unpaid overtime.

---

## What success looks like in three months

By the end of the first quarter, success means:

- **Every resident in the building has an account.** Not 80%. Not "most." Every one — because a community tool only works at critical mass.
- **At least 20 complaints have been submitted, tracked, and resolved** with a visible status trail. Residents can see their complaint moved from *Open* to *Resolved* without chasing anyone.
- **The OC has retired the building WhatsApp group** as the primary communication channel. Announcements go through the portal. Meeting minutes are posted and searchable.
- **Zero complaints about privacy or data leaks.** Anonymous complaints stay anonymous. Personal data stays in Australia. Residents trust the system because it has never given them a reason not to.
- **The OC chair can generate a quarterly report in under five minutes** — not a weekend.

If all five are true, the app has done its job. If any one is false, that is the next thing to fix.
